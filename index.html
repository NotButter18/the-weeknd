ORIGINS

Abel Makkonen Tesfaye

Born in Toronto on February 16, 1990, Abel is the child of Ethiopian immigrants and grew up speaking Amharic. He works with many other big artists and is signed to his own label, XO Records. He often collaborates with Ariana Grande and producer Metro Boomin. He is currently dating Simi Khadra.

After a hard childhood, he dropped out of high school and left his home. He packed his mattress into a friend's van one weekend and never came back, which is where his name, The Weeknd, comes from (he removed the "e" for legal reasons).

His Sound

He is known for his own dark style of R&B with moody and deep beats and honest lyrics. Over his career, he has mixed different styles of music, moving from dark R&B into dreamy pop and big '80s style songs. He uses old-school keyboards to make his music feel like a movie.

He is completely self-taught and has no formal musical training. He developed his amazing high voice and songwriting skills by listening to his musical heroes and trying new things in Toronto studios.

LEGACY & INFLUENCES

Who Influenced Him

His biggest vocal and performance influence by far is Michael Jackson. He also draws heavy inspiration from Prince, alternative rock bands like the Deftones, and renowned Ethiopian singers like Aster Aweke.

Our Personal Opinion

We think how he went from an unknown singer to a global superstar while keeping his dark style is one of the coolest things in music. We are casual listeners, but we respect how his amazing high voice and high-quality production changed how pop music sounds today. In our opinion, his best strength is making radio hits feel like deep stories instead of just normal pop songs. We chose him because his journey from the streets of Toronto to the Super Bowl stage is the best example of how an artist can grow.

His Impact on Music

The Weeknd's biggest impact on music was making "Alternative R&B" popular. Before him, most R&B was just smooth and traditional. He proved that you could make R&B that was dark and moody, and it could still be a massive hit on the radio.

He changed modern pop music and helped other artists like Billie Eilish and Post Malone find their own style.
