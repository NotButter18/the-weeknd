ORIGINS

Abel Makkonen Tesfaye

Born in Toronto on February 16, 1990, and raised in the city's Scarborough district. He is the only child of Ethiopian immigrant parents. His Ethiopian heritage deeply shapes his identity, and he grew up speaking Amharic as his first language.

He famously dropped out of high school, packed his mattress, and left home one weekend and never came back—inspiring his stage name (he dropped the "e" to avoid copyright issues with an existing band).

The Sound & Evolution

Known for popularizing a signature Dark R&B sound featuring moody, atmospheric beats and vulnerable lyrics. Over his career, he has seamlessly blended genres, moving from mysterious alternative R&B into grand Dream Pop and massive '80s Synth-Pop, relying on vintage synthesizers and cinematic production.

Entirely self-taught without formal classical training, he developed his incredible falsetto voice, pitch, and songwriting skills by listening to his idols and experimenting in underground Toronto studios.

LEGACY & INFLUENCES

Who Influenced Him

His biggest vocal and performance influence by far is Michael Jackson. He also draws heavy inspiration from Prince, alternative rock bands like the Deftones, and renowned Ethiopian singers like Aster Aweke.

My Personal Opinion

To be honest, I am more of a casual listener when it comes to his older music, but it is impossible to ignore his massive impact on the industry. My absolute favorite song is his recent collaboration "Timeless" with Playboi Carti. The beat is incredibly catchy, and I love how his smooth vocals sound on modern rap production. I chose to research him for this presentation because even if you aren't a die-hard fan, you have to respect how he went from an anonymous underground singer in Toronto to the biggest pop star on the planet.

Contribution to Music History

The Weeknd's biggest contribution to music history is mainstreaming "Alternative R&B." Before him, mainstream R&B was mostly smooth and traditional. He proved that you could make R&B that was edgy, dark, and highly cinematic, and it could still dominate global pop radio.

He reshaped modern pop and R&B, paving the way for artists like Billie Eilish, Khalid, 6LACK, Post Malone, and countless aspects of modern hip-hop and pop production.
